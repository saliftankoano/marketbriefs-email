Sunny Delight
With AI on the rise, tech companies are looking to fill big energy needs to power new data centers.
A lot of that energy will come from the fastest-growing energy sector: Solar power.
  • The government says that solar power will make up 58% of all new energy generation in the U.S. in 2024.
It’s cheaper: The price of installing solar production has fallen below virtually every other method - though storing energy in batteries remains expensive.
40% of demand for solar projects in the U.S. over the last five years came from just four companies: Amazon, Google, Meta, and Microsoft.
Strong and Slick
Another popular form of energy, oil, recovered this week.
Oil prices rose to their highest level in seven weeks on Wednesday. Experts expect summer gas demand to exceed initial expectations.
  • Still, conflicts overseas and production concerns continue to keep the industry on its toes.

Vote Of (No) Confidence
Toyota Chairman Akio Toyoda recently got a 71.9% approval rating from his company’s shareholders.
That’s a good enough number to pass your math test - but in the Japanese corporate world, it’s really really low.
  • Japanese board members regularly get nearly 100% support from their shareholders.
Toyota recently got in trouble for cheating on its safety tests on seven of its models, with Toyoda apologizing for the fraud.
Though Toyoda will remain chairman, the vote shows he may have resistance.
The Great British Rate Off
The U.K. hit its 2% inflation target in May - but experts think the Bank of England will keep interest rates where they are for now.
Inflation has walloped the U.K., with the country having not hit its 2% target in three years.
  • The U.K. is taking a more cautious approach than the E.U., which has already lowered rates earlier this June.
Still, experts are forecasting a cut in August, with the inflation numbers lifting the London stock market over the past week.

The Big Reveal
